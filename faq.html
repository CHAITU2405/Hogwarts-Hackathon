Commonly Asked Questions
Can we switch our domain/track after selecting the problem statement?
No. Once you choose your problem statement between 06:00 AM – 06:00 PM, you must continue with it. Choose carefully.
Can we submit our GitHub repository privately?
Make it public or add the organizers/judges as collaborators. If judges cannot access your repo, your submission may not be evaluated.
Do all team members need to be present for the entire hackathon?
Yes. Since it's mentioned all wizards/witches must stay in the Great Hall the whole time, no one can leave for long durations unless informed to organizers.
How do we prove our work is original and done during the hackathon?
Keep your commits visible on GitHub. Judges may check timestamps. Copy-paste code or pre-built projects may lead to disqualification.
How do we contact organizers for emergency help?
Approach the nearest volunteer (as per rules) — not other teams.
Do we need to submit a video demo?
If required, organizers will mention it. If not, prepare one as a backup — it helps judges understand your project quickly.
What if two teams have similar ideas?
Judging depends on execution, innovation, and implementation, so similar ideas are allowed.
